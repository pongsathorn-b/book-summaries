# The Intelligence Trap: Why Smart People Make Dumb Mistakes

> "Your potential is shaped by your daily habits and the frameworks you use to understand yourself and the world."

## Why I Read It

Highly educated, accomplished people still make terrible decisions. Understanding why is both humbling and practically useful.

## Key Takeaways

- Intelligence amplifies cognitive biases — smarter people rationalize more confidently
- Expertise creates blind spots: the more you know, the less you question your domain
- Active open-mindedness: actively seek out information that contradicts your beliefs
- Intellectual humility: knowing the limits of your knowledge is wisdom
- Dumb decisions in smart people often come from overconfidence in System 1 thinking

## Actionable Lessons

- When making a major decision, write down your reasoning before deciding
- Seek out a 'red team' who will actively challenge your plan
- Adopt the 'outsider test': what would a smart, uninvolved friend advise?
- Use structured decision frameworks (pros/cons, decision trees) before trusting intuition
- Sleep before major decisions — fatigue increases reliance on System 1
