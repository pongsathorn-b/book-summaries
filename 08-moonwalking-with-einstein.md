# Moonwalking with Einstein — Joshua Foer

> "We forget things because we don't pay attention, not because we have bad memories."

## Overview

[[Joshua Foer]] wanted to understand how memory works — and accidentally became a USA Memory Championship winner in one year. This book is his journey into the world of [[Memory Athletes]], ancient techniques, and the nature of memory itself.

## The [[Forgetting Curve]]

[[Hermann Ebbinghaus]] discovered that we forget roughly 50% of new information within an hour, 70% within 24 hours. [[Memory is Fragile]] — forgetting is the default, not the exception.

## [[Memory Athletes Are Made, Not Born]]

The world memory champions aren't geniuses — they're people who trained using ancient techniques. [[Extraordinary Recall]] is available to anyone.

## Key Techniques

### [[Memory Palace]] (Method of Loci)

The most powerful memory technique known. Visualize a familiar place, place vivid, bizarre mental images at specific locations, then mentally walk through to recall.

- Used by ancient Roman orators, medieval scholars, modern [[Memory Athletes]]
- More bizarre and vivid = more memorable

### [[Attention]] Is the Gateway
You cannot remember what you never paid attention to.

### [[Emotion and Memory]]
Emotionally charged events are remembered better. [[Fear]], humor, surprise are powerful triggers.

### [[Visualization]]
Vivid, multisensory, bizarre images stick better than bland ones.

## [[The Memory Palace Network]]

Expert [[Memory Athletes]] use dozens — hundreds — of linked [[Memory Palaces]]. This allows remembering thousands of items in order.
