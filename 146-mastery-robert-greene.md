# Mastery

> "Your potential is shaped by your daily habits and the frameworks you use to understand yourself and the world."

## Why I Read It

Understanding how historical masters — from Darwin to Mozart to Einstein — reached the top of their fields provides a template anyone can follow.

## Key Takeaways

- Follow your inclinations — the unique pattern of your interests reveals your calling
- The apprenticeship phase (1–10 years): observe, absorb, practice under a mentor
- Creative absorption: study the masters so deeply their thinking becomes unconscious
- The social intelligence of masters: understand power dynamics, navigate resistance
- Masters never stop being apprentices — always deepening, always learning

## Actionable Lessons

- Identify who has achieved what you want and reverse-engineer their path
- Say yes to assignments that expand your skill range, not just comfort zone
- Keep a 'craftsman's journal': daily notes on what you learned in your field
- Find a mentor — even informal mentorship accelerates mastery dramatically
- Build social intelligence: observe who supports and who undercuts you
