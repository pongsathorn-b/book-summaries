# Mindset: The New Psychology of Success

> "Your potential is shaped by your daily habits and the frameworks you use to understand yourself and the world."

## Why I Read It

Drawn in by the idea that your beliefs about intelligence and ability shape your trajectory more than raw talent. The distinction between fixed and growth mindsets felt like a fundamental reframe.

## Key Takeaways

- Fixed mindset: believes talent alone creates success — failure is devastating
- Growth mindset: believes abilities can be developed through effort — failure is information
- Praise effort and strategy, not intelligence or talent
- Challenges are opportunities to learn, not threats to self-worth
- Even 'natural' geniuses must grow through struggle

## Actionable Lessons

- Replace 'I'm naturally bad at X' with 'I'm still learning X'
- When you fail, ask 'What did I learn?' not 'Am I talented enough?'
- Give growth-oriented feedback: 'You worked through that difficulty well'
- Choose challenges over easy wins — growth lives at the edge of comfort
- Model a growth mindset for children: celebrate effort, not just results
