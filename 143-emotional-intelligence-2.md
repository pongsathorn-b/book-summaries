# Emotional Intelligence 2.0

> "Your potential is shaped by your daily habits and the frameworks you use to understand yourself and the world."

## Why I Read It

EI often matters more than IQ in leadership and relationships. Wanted a practical, actionable framework rather than academic theory.

## Key Takeaways

- EI has four skills: self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship management
- Self-awareness: know your emotions, strengths, blind spots, and values
- Self-management: stay calm, adaptable, and focused under pressure
- Social awareness: read room dynamics, show genuine empathy
- Relationship management: resolve conflict, influence others, collaborate

## Actionable Lessons

- Take the self-assessment and identify your lowest EI skill first
- Practice label emotions specifically — 'irritated' not just 'bad feeling'
- Pause before reacting — 6 seconds allows the rational brain to engage
- Mirror others' body language to build social rapport
- Give criticism as 'observed behavior + impact' — not judgment
