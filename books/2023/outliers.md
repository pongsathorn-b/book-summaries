# Outliers

## Summary

Malcolm Gladwell examines what separates high-achievers from the rest, arguing that success is less about talent than about opportunity, timing, and 10,000 hours of deliberate practice.

## Key Takeaways

- 10,000 hours of deliberate practice is a necessary but not sufficient condition for mastery.
- Opportunity, cultural legacy, and timing determine success as much as talent.
- IQ tests measure a narrow band of intelligence and miss many forms of brilliance.
