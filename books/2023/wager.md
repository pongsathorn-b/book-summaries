# The Wager: A Tale of Shipwreck, Mutiny and Murder

## Summary

David Grann tells the story of a shipwrecked crew's ordeal on a desert island and the subsequent court-martial that became a window into 18th-century British naval justice.

## Key Takeaways

- Survival under extreme conditions reveals the elasticity of human morality.
- The legal system of the 1700s used courts-martial to project naval discipline.
- Grann's investigative approach makes history feel like a thriller.
