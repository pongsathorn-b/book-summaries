# Born a Crime

## Summary

Trevor Noah's memoir about growing up in South Africa as the child of a Black mother and White Swiss father during apartheid is both hilarious and harrowing, tracing how humor became his survival tool.

## Key Takeaways

- Humor can be a survival strategy in oppressive systems.
- Identity is shaped by context as much as by individual choice.
- South Africa's transition from apartheid was more complex than a simple liberation story.
