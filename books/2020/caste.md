# Caste: The Origins of Our Discontents

## Summary

Isabel Wilkerson reframes American racism as a caste system with deep historical roots, drawing parallels to India and Nazi Germany to illuminate the invisible architecture of inequality.

## Key Takeaways

- America's racial hierarchy functions as a caste system with invisible enforcement mechanisms.
- The middle and upper classes benefit from the existence of a permanent underclass.
- Caste thinking is adaptive — it can be unlearned but requires active effort.
