# A Promised Land

## Summary

Barack Obama's first presidential memoir covers his early political career through his first term, revealing the calculus and moral weight behind major foreign and domestic decisions.

## Key Takeaways

- Presidential decision-making involves trade-offs that no campaign fully prepares you for.
- Bipartisan governance requires personal relationships across the aisle that are now nearly extinct.
- Grace under pressure is both a character trait and a cultivated skill.
