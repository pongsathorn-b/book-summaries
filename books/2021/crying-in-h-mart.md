# Crying in H Mart

## Summary

Michelle Zauner of Japanese Breakfast uses the lens of Korean food culture to explore grief, identity, and what it means to belong to a family that is being slowly erased by illness.

## Key Takeaways

- Food is memory, identity, and connection — especially when loss is involved.
- Grief doesn't resolve; it changes shape as we learn to carry it.
- Mixed-race identity involves a particular kind of invisible grief and belonging.
