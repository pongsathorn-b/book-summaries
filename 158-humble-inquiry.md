# Humble Inquiry: The Gentle Art of Asking Instead of Telling

> "Your potential is shaped by your daily habits and the frameworks you use to understand yourself and the world."

## Why I Read It

Most people — especially in leadership — default to telling. The cost is hidden information, defensive behavior, and poor collaboration.

## Key Takeaways

- Humble Inquiry = asking questions to which you don't already know the answer
- Telling creates dependency, compliance, and hiding — asking creates commitment
- Helpful questions are genuinely open — not leading, not diagnostic
- Build relationship before task — the quality of connection determines communication quality
- Curiosity is the foundation of humility — genuine interest in the other person

## Actionable Lessons

- Replace 'You should...' with 'Help me understand...'
- Practice 70% questions, 30% statements in your next team meeting
- When someone shares a problem, ask 'What have you tried?' before offering solutions
- Notice when you feel an urge to tell — pause and turn it into a question
- Follow up with silence after asking — resist the urge to fill the space
