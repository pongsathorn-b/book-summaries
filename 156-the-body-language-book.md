# The Definitive Book of Body Language

> "Your potential is shaped by your daily habits and the frameworks you use to understand yourself and the world."

## Why I Read It

Over 70% of communication is nonverbal, yet most people never study it. Understanding body language sharpens leadership, negotiation, and social connection.

## Key Takeaways

- Posture communicates status: expansive = high status, contracted = low status
- Mirroring builds rapport unconsciously — people like those who copy them
- The 'double zap': verbal and nonverbal messages must agree for trust
- The distance zones: intimate (0-45cm), personal (45-120cm), social (1.2-3.5m), public (3.5m+)
- The feet never lie — they're the most honest part of the body

## Actionable Lessons

- In meetings, mirror the other person's posture slightly and pace
- Maintain eye contact 60-70% of the time — more feels aggressive, less feels disengaged
- Match your arm/leg positions to your conversation partner's to build trust
- Notice when someone's verbal and nonverbal cues contradict
- Use 'impression management' consciously: power poses before high-stakes situations
