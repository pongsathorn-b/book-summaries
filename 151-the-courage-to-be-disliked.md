# The Courage to Be Disliked

> "Your potential is shaped by your daily habits and the frameworks you use to understand yourself and the world."

## Why I Read It

The idea that you can be free of your past by changing your current interpretation — not your circumstances — felt radical and immediately usable.

## Key Takeaways

- Your past doesn't cause your current problems — your current purpose does
- Separation of tasks: know what is your responsibility vs. others'
- Freedom is being disliked by others — stop seeking approval
- Contribution to others is the measure of self-worth — not achievements
- Life is not a narrative you 'live' but a series of present moments you choose

## Actionable Lessons

- When feeling anxious about others' opinions, ask 'Is this their task or mine?'
- Practice contributing first — value follows contribution, not the reverse
- Reframe past failures as 'what did they teach me?' not 'what do they mean about me?'
- Make one decision per day without worrying about others' approval
- Recognize 'lifestyle' habits — are you performing for others unconsciously?
