# The Righteous Mind: Why Good People Are Divided by Politics and Religion

> "Your potential is shaped by your daily habits and the frameworks you use to understand yourself and the world."

## Why I Read It

Understanding why smart, good people disagree on moral and political issues is essential for navigating a polarized world.

## Key Takeaways

- Intuitions come first, strategic reasoning second — we think with our gut
- Six moral foundations: Care, Fairness, Loyalty, Authority, Sanctity, Liberty
- Progressives use Care, Fairness, Liberty more; Conservatives use all six equally
- The 'Hive' switch: religion and group rituals bind people into superorganisms
- Moral foundations are toolkit: different cultures develop different moral diets

## Actionable Lessons

- Before arguing, ask: which moral foundation is the other person defending?
- Practice 'principled disagreement' — understand the opposing view before critiquing
- Recognize that tribalism is an innate instinct, not just a political choice
- Build 'moral dumbfounding' awareness — when you can't explain your gut reaction
- Seek moral diversity in your circles — different intuitions create robust ethics
