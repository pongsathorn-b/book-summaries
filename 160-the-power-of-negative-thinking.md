# The Power of Negative Thinking

> "Your potential is shaped by your daily habits and the frameworks you use to understand yourself and the world."

## Why I Read It

Positive thinking often produces fragile optimism. Knight's approach — confront reality, prepare obsessively, manage anxiety — felt more honest.

## Key Takeaways

- Prepare for what can go wrong — not for what will go right
- Positive thinking without preparation is delusion; realistic concern drives focus
- Mental rehearsal: visualize failure scenarios and planned responses
- Discipline and structure create freedom — chaos and permissiveness create anxiety
- The 'worst-case scenario' drill: face your fear of failure to defuse it

## Actionable Lessons

- In planning, explicitly ask: 'What could go wrong? What's our contingency?'
- Use anxiety productively: channel fear into focused, deliberate preparation
- Set realistic expectations — optimism without basis creates fragility
- Practice visualizing failure and recovery — build resilience through mental rehearsal
- Create and enforce structure — routines reduce cognitive load under pressure
