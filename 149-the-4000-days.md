# The 4000 Weeks: Time Management for Mortals

> "Your potential is shaped by your daily habits and the frameworks you use to understand yourself and the world."

## Why I Read It

The productivity genre often avoids the fundamental constraint: time is finite and death approaches. This book faces that honestly.

## Key Takeaways

- Time management is an illusion — you cannot get control of time, only your relationship to it
- The 'serenity prayer' for productivity: focus on what you can change, accept what you can't
- Limit your to-do list: embrace radical omission — what will you deliberately NOT do?
- Presence is the highest use of time — being fully in the now, not just efficient
- Your life is not a project to be optimized but an experience to be inhabited

## Actionable Lessons

- Ask 'If this is the only thing I do today, does it matter?' before choosing tasks
- Confront your mortality weekly — 'memento Mori' journaling
- Practice 'productive procrastination' — deliberately do something unimportant instead
- Stop trying to clear your mind; let thoughts arise and pass without attachment
- Say no to good opportunities to protect space for great ones
