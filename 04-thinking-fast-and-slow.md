# Thinking, Fast and Slow — Daniel Kahneman

> "We're confident even when we're wrong."

## Overview

Nobel Prize-winning psychologist [[Daniel Kahneman]] reveals the two systems that drive how we think: [[System 1]] (fast, intuitive, emotional) and [[System 2]] (slow, deliberate, logical). Most of our decisions come from System 1 — and that's where the trouble starts.

## The Two Systems

### [[System 1]]: Fast Thinking
- Automatic, intuitive, effortless
- Makes snap judgments, sees patterns that aren't there
- Prone to [[Cognitive Biases]] and logical errors

### [[System 2]]: Slow Thinking
- Deliberate, effortful, logical
- Activated when [[System 1]] hits its limits
- Lazy — often accepts [[System 1]]'s answers without checking

## Key Biases

### [[Anchoring Bias]]
The first number you see anchors your judgment.

### [[Availability Heuristic]]
Things easier to recall seem more common. [[Media Influence]] on [[Risk Perception]].

### [[Loss Aversion]]
Losses loom larger than equivalent gains. Loss feels ~2x more painful than gain feels good.

### The Planning Fallacy
We consistently underestimate costs, risks, and time needed. [[Overconfidence]].

### The [[Halo Effect]]
One positive trait bleeds into everything. [[Reputation]] affects judgment.

## What "Thinking" Really Means

Most of our "thinking" is actually [[System 1]] — a prediction machine, not a logic machine. [[System 2]] is lazy and largely accepts [[System 1]]'s output.

## For Founders

- [[First Impressions]] of products and founders are [[System 1]] judgments
- [[A/B Testing]] reveals what [[System 2]] actually prefers vs what [[System 1]] says
- [[Investor Decisions]] are heavily influenced by [[Anchoring Bias]] and [[Halo Effect]]
- [[Forecasting]] requires engaging [[System 2]] and challenging [[System 1]]'s quick conclusions
