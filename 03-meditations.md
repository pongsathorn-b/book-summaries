# Meditations — Marcus Aurelius

## Overview

Private journal of the Roman Emperor Marcus Aurelius, written as personal philosophy and self-reminder. Not meant for publication — raw, direct Stoic thinking from the 2nd century AD. One of the most read books in history by a head of state.

## Core Philosophy: Stoicism

Stoicism is not about suppressing emotion — it's about distinguishing between what you control and what you don't. Focus energy only on what you can change.

## Key Concepts

### The Dichotomy of Control
- You control only your own thoughts and actions
- You don't control what happens to you — only your response
- External events are neither good nor bad — they simply are

> "You have power over your mind — not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength." — Marcus Aurelius

### The View From Above
When facing difficulty, zoom out:
- This problem is tiny in the context of the universe
- This moment will pass
- Think of the problem 100 years from now — meaningless

### Amor Fati — Love of Fate
Accept what happens. Not resignation — embracing. Whatever occurs, treat it as an opportunity to act virtuously.

> "A blazing fire makes flame and brightness out of everything that is thrown into it."

### The Present Moment
Only this moment exists. The past is gone, the future uncertain. Dwelling causes suffering.

> "Waste no more time arguing about what a good man should be. Be one."

### Virtue Is the Only Good
Material success, health, reputation — all indifferent. Only virtue matters: wisdom, justice, courage, temperance.

### Detachment
Do your work without attachment to results. The outcome is not yours to control.

## Key Lessons

1. **Respond, don't react** — between stimulus and response, there is space. Use it.
2. **Focus on service** — what can you do for others? The ego shrinks when focused on something larger.
3. **Memento mori** — you will die. Live accordingly. What matters today?
4. **Hard circumstances create strength** — fire tempers steel.
5. **No one can hurt you without your consent** — hurt comes from judgment, not the event.

## For Founders

- Crisis is inevitable — your response to it is always a choice
- The emperor was a leader of 50 million people and still wrote daily reminders about humility — stay grounded regardless of success
- "The impediment to action advances it. What gets in the way becomes the way." — obstacles are fuel

## One-Line Takeaway

> You can't control what happens to you — only your thoughts and actions. Choose them wisely.
