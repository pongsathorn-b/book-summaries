# Meditations — Marcus Aurelius

> "You have power over your mind — not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength."

## Overview

Private journal of Roman Emperor [[Marcus Aurelius]], written as personal philosophy and self-reminder. Not meant for publication — raw Stoic thinking from the 2nd century AD. One of the most read books in history by a head of state.

## Core Philosophy: [[Stoicism]]

[[Stoicism]] is not about suppressing emotion — it's about distinguishing between what you control and what you don't. Focus energy only on what you can change.

## Key Concepts

### The [[Dichotomy of Control]]
- You control only your own thoughts and actions
- You don't control what happens — only your response
- External events are neither good nor bad — they simply are

### [[Amor Fati]] — Love of Fate
Accept what happens. Not resignation — embracing. Whatever occurs, treat it as an opportunity.

### The View From Above
When facing difficulty, zoom out: this problem is tiny in the context of the universe.

### [[Memento Mori]]
You will die. Live accordingly. What matters today?

### Virtue Is the Only Good
Material success, health, reputation — all indifferent. Only [[Virtue]] matters: [[Wisdom]], [[Justice]], [[Courage]], [[Temperance]].

## Key Lessons

1. **Respond, don't react** — between stimulus and response, there is space
2. **Focus on service** — what can you do for others?
3. **Hard circumstances create strength** — [[Obstacles as Fuel]]
4. **No one can hurt you without your consent** — hurt comes from judgment, not the event
