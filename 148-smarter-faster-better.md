# Smarter, Faster, Better: The Secrets of Being Productive in Life and Business

> "Your potential is shaped by your daily habits and the frameworks you use to understand yourself and the world."

## Why I Read It

Follow-up to The Power of Habit that goes deeper into the cognitive and structural drivers of high performance.

## Key Takeaways

- Mental models help: understanding why you do something beats just managing it
- Psychological safety: teams that trust each other outperform on every metric
- Make a task ' cognitive drawing board' — externalizing thoughts reduces cognitive load
- Stretch goals with specific constraints drive more creativity than vague ones
- Active planning: mentally rehearse obstacles and pre-decide responses

## Actionable Lessons

- Before each day, write down your top 3 goals and one key obstacle for each
- Build psychological safety in teams: share mistakes, invite dissent
- Use 'if-then' planning: 'If X happens, then I will do Y'
- Externalize your thinking with a notebook — free your brain for higher-order thinking
- Take breaks that restore focus: walk, stretch, change environments
