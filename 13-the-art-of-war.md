# The Art of War — Sun Tzu

> "Know yourself and you will win all battles. Know the enemy and you need not fear the result of a hundred battles."

## Overview

Written in the 5th century BCE by Chinese military strategist [[Sun Tzu]]. Originally for winning wars — now required reading for business, sports, and negotiation. The core thesis: the highest form of generalship is to [[Balk the Enemy's Plans]]. Don't fight if you can avoid it. Win before you fight.

## Key Principles

### [[Know Yourself, Know the Enemy]]

Most people study competitors — but [[Sun Tzu]] says study yourself with equal intensity.

### [[The Art of Deception]]

Appear weak when strong. Appear strong when weak. The best victories are ones where the enemy never saw the battle coming.

### [[Win Before You Fight]]

The supreme [[Military Strategy]] is to [[Subdue Without Fighting]]. In startups: [[Disruptive Market Entry]] by creating markets where no competition exists.

### [[Strategic Positioning]]

[[Control High Ground]] — position matters more than execution. In business: [[Product-Market Fit]] is positioning.

### [[Speed]]

Speed creates its own advantages. [[Seize Opportunities]] before the enemy realizes they exist.

### [[Use Enemy's Strength Against Them]]

[[Turn Disaster to Advantage]]. What seems like an obstacle becomes a tool.

## The Five Factors

1. **[[The Way]]** — shared purpose between leader and team
2. **[[The Sky]]** — [[Timing and Season]] (right timing)
3. **[[The Ground]]** — [[Terrain]] and [[Chokepoints]]
4. **[[The Command]]** — [[Leadership Quality]]
5. **[[The Method]]** — [[Organization]] and [[Logistics]]

## For Founders

- [[Win Before You Fight]] — avoid [[Head-On Competition]] where possible
- [[Speed and Information Advantage]] are the modern equivalent of terrain and spies
- [[The General Is Everything]] — [[Founder Quality]] matters more than anything else
